Gridfinity finally made it to the kitchen. I printed The Next Layer's complete kitchen collection — bins for forks, knives, spoons, chopsticks, straws, and the kids' cutlery — and it's one of those projects that makes you wonder why you waited so long.

## The Build

Printed on the Bambu Lab P1S. The collection is big, so this was a multi-session print job. The designs are chunky by intention — thick walls to handle the daily abuse of being yanked out of a drawer by a seven-year-old — and that shows in print time, but also in how solid everything feels.

One gap in the collection: no knife tray with a 2x6 footprint and no internal divider. The existing knife bins didn't fit my drawer layout, so I remixed my own in OnShape. It'll go up on Printables once it's cleaned up and shared.

## Result

The drawer that used to be an archaeological dig for the right spatula now makes sense. Everything has a place, the finger slides line up, and the modular blanks fill the gaps cleanly. The knife tray remix works exactly as needed.
